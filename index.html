What we do

Professionally trained volunteers, ready for the call.

Our team trains year-round to support the RCMP, municipal agencies and neighbouring SAR teams with safe and effective ground search and rescue operations in all seasons and terrain.

Why your gift matters

Your donation keeps volunteers safe and equipped.

As a not-for-profit charity, we rely on grants and community donations to fund training, maintain vehicles and purchase specialized equipment for rescues in the mountains and foothills.

Every search depends on reliable gear and well-trained people: radios and GPS units, medical kits, avalanche safety equipment, and our mobile command resources.

Cochrane Search and Rescue Association is a registered Canadian charity (BN 86762 2110 RR0001). Tax receipts are issued for eligible donations.

Volunteer

Join a team that trains hard and looks after each other.

Members supply their own basic outdoor gear and commit time to meetings, training nights and callouts. In return, you gain new skills, experience and the chance to help people on their worst days.

How to become a member

  • 1
    Review our member expectations for availability, training, and fitness.
  • 2
    Submit an application and attend an information session with our membership team.
  • 3
    Complete initial training and mentoring to become an operational member.

Our service area

We respond primarily in Cochrane, Canmore, Kananaskis and surrounding backcountry, and may assist neighbouring Alberta SAR teams when requested.

Our history

2001
CSAR founded to provide trained Ground SAR for the Cochrane region.
2010s
Team grows to ~75 members and expands training in wilderness search, rope rescue and winter operations.
2020s
Increased call volume and new partnerships to support a growing region west of Calgary.
